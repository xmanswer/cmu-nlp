For Friday: 	WASHINGTON NEWS (``w'' category) 	KEMP-ISRAEL-PROBE (Washington) _ Former Republican vice presidential nominee Jack Kemp is proposing that an organization he heads investigate charges that Israeli forces massacred Palestinians in the West Bank city of Jenin. Kemp's plan follows a U.N. decision not to conduct the probe itself. By DAN FREEDMAN. 	COMMENTARY (``k'' category) 	MEANS-COLUMN (Washington) _ As a team, Sens. John Kerry, D-Mass., and John McCain, R-Ariz., create good political theater. They have worked closely together on issues such as normalizing relations with Vietnam, where they both fought. They have become fast friends and they each harbor thoughts of winning the White House. By MARIANNE MEANS. 	For Sunday: 	WASHINGTON NEWS (``w'' category) 	HELMS-HOMOSEXUALS (Washington) _ Gays, lesbians and AIDS activists are welcoming a new ally _ Sen. Jesse Helms. The senior senator from North Carolina, an arch conservative, has made a career out of berating homosexuals and blocking federal AIDS funding. Now he is their most valuable player in lobbying for federal dollars to fight the disease in Africa. The ailing 30-year Senate veteran, who retires this year, wants his swan song to be his effort to stop the transmission of AIDS from mother to child in Africa. By JUDY HOLLAND. 	COURT-DECISIONS (Washington) _ As the Supreme Court nears the end of its term in June, the justices still face many tough decisions, including school vouchers and how far states can go to protect to the rights of HMO patients. We examine the major cases remaining on the court's agenda and the possible impact of their decisions on the country. By MARK HELM. 	COMMENTARY (``k'' category) 	THOMPSON-COLUMN (Washington) _ The planned meeting between President Bush and the reluctant Israeli prime minister, Ariel Sharon, next week should tell us something about prospects for peace in the Middle East. Sharon and Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat are equally guilty in blocking promising accords. By ROBERT E. THOMPSON.
