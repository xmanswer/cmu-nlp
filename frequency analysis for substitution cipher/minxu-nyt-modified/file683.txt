SECURE-HIGHRISE (Los Angeles) -- Evacuation procedures and private security measures can be improved at high-rise buildings in Los Angeles, according to a RAND commissioned study released Thursday in the wake of the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks. Although high-rises don't face as large a threat as Los Angeles International Airport and the harbor, buildings can increase surveillance and limit access, according to the report released by city officials. Councilman Jack Weiss will introduce an ordinance to require twice-a-year evacuation drills in tall buildings. By Rick Orlov. (Will move on the ``a'' national and ``n'' California news wires.)
