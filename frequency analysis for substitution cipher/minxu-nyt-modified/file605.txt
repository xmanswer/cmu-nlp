Thursday, May 2, 2002 	Editors 	The Hearst News Service will file the following for Friday and Sunday. If you have questions, please call Susie McBee or Tim Spence at 202-298-6920. 	(Adds: CONGRESS-MIDEAST; BUSH-EUROPE; ENRON-CUTS; WORKERS-INSURANCE; APACHE-CHIEF; ECONOMIC-OUTLOOK; POWER-STRUGGLE; and EARTH-WATCH.) 	(The ``HNS'' tag appears in all sluglines.) 	For Friday: 	WASHINGTON NEWS (``w'' category) 	CONGRESS-MIDEAST (Washington) _ The House, led by Majority Whip Tom DeLay, moves to approve a strongly worded pro-Israel resolution despite concerns from the White House that it could hurt U.S. relations with Arab countries. A companion Senate resolution has more middle ground. By KAREN MASTERSON. 	KEMP-ISRAEL-PROBE (Washington) _ Former Republican vice presidential nominee Jack Kemp is proposing that an organization he heads investigate charges that Israeli forces massacred Palestinians in the Jenin refugee camp in the West Bank. Kemp's plan follows a U.N. decision abandon its own probe amid Israel objections. By DAN FREEDMAN. 	BUSH-EUROPE (Washington) _ President Bush meets with European Union leaders on Mideast issues, terrorism and trade. By BENNETT ROTH. 	BUSINESS/FINANCIAL NEWS (``f'' category) 	ENRON-CUTS (Houston) _ Enron's reorganization appears to call for more massive job cuts. By LAURA GOLDBERG. 	WORKERS-INSURANCE (Washington) _ Rep. Gene Green, D-Texas, and House Democratic Whip Nancy Pelosi of California file a bill that would require companies to disclose life insurance policies they take out on their workers. By JULIE MASON. 	APACHE-CHIEF (Houston) _ Apache Corporation Chairman Raymond Plank announces that G. Steven Farris has been elected chief executive officer, president and chief operating officer effective May 29. Plank, the energy company's founder, announced the change at Apache's annual meeting and said he will continue to participate in the management of the company. By BILL HENSEL. 	ECONOMIC-OUTLOOK (Houston) _ Despite the collapse of Enron, economic conditions in Texas' largest city continue to outperform the rest of the nation. But the city will fall behind by year's end, a prominent Houston economist says. By RALPH BIVINS. 	POWER-STRUGGLE (Houston) _ Consumer groups filed a petition asking the Public Utility Commission to protect New Power customers from being dumped to the more expensive providers if the struggling New Power should cease operations in Texas. By DAVID KAPLAN. 	NATIONAL NEWS (``a'' category) 	EARTH-WATCH (Houston) _ NASA readies a new environmental satellite called Aqua for launching early Saturday from Vandenberg Air Force Base, Calif. Aqua is the centerpiece of a near $1 billion, six-year study of how water cycles between the land and the ocean and the atmosphere and the influences the process has on climate and the environment. By MARK CARREAU. 	COMMENTARY (``k'' category) 	MEANS-COLUMN (Washington) _ As a team, Sens. John Kerry, D-Mass., and John McCain, R-Ariz., create good political theater. They have worked closely together on issues such as normalizing relations with Vietnam, where they both fought. They have become fast friends and they each harbor thoughts of winning the White House. By MARIANNE MEANS. 	For Sunday: 	WASHINGTON NEWS (``w'' category) 	HELMS-HOMOSEXUALS (Washington) _ Gays, lesbians and AIDS activists are welcoming a new ally _ Republican Sen. Jesse Helms. The senior senator from North Carolina has made a career out of berating homosexuals and blocking federal AIDS funding. Now he is their most valuable player in lobbying for federal dollars to fight the disease in Africa. The ailing 30-year Senate veteran, who retires this year, wants his swan song to be his effort to stop the transmission of AIDS from mother to child in Africa. By JUDY HOLLAND. 	COURT-DECISIONS (Washington) _ As the Supreme Court nears the end of its term in June, the justices still face many tough decisions, including school vouchers and how far states can go to protect to the rights of HMO patients. We examine the major cases remaining on the court's agenda and the possible impact of their decisions on the country. By MARK HELM. 	COMMENTARY (``k'' category) 	THOMPSON-COLUMN (Washington) _ The planned meeting between President Bush and Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon next week should tell us something about prospects for peace in the Middle East. Sharon and Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat are equally guilty in blocking promising accords. By ROBERT E. THOMPSON.
