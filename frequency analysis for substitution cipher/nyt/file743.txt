LINDBERGH-FLIGHT (Undated) _ He braved high-altitude storms, cramping fatigue, his grandfather's legacy and at the end, the French press. But Erik Lindbergh couldn't keep the smile from his face after he landed at La Bourget Airport 18 hours after he took off from New York in a single-engine aircraft to celebrate the 75th anniversary of Charles Lindbergh's famous transatlantic flight. Calling his flight a ``celebration not a recreation'' Erick had a faster, lighter stronger craft with far superior navigation and weather capabilities. And the advantages didn't stop there. To get home, an exhausted Erik admitted with a laugh, ``I'm going commercial, all the way.'' 	By Mike Lewis. (Story will move in the ``i'' category.)
